About The Meyers-Briggs Type Indicator

Meyers-Briggs Type Indicator, or MBTI for short, was developed by a mother/daugher team of psycologists in the 1940's, working off of Carl Jung's typological theory. It consists of a binary system of 4 traits, that combine to make a total of 16 personality types.

In every society the proportions of each type in the general population are varied and some types appear to be quite common, and others very rare.
